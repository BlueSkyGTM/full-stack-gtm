Why this exists

01
Zero active recall

Retrieval practice is how lessons stick — not re-reading, not passive watching. Professor Synapse fires the concept at you and makes you apply it on the spot. Before he explains anything, he asks what you already think. That's not a personality quirk. That's the whole point.

02
Claude Code native — built lean

This runs in Claude Code. Every session is scoped to your current phase and progress — nothing else. No inflated system prompts. The design is intentional: do more per token, not more tokens.

03
Always the right expert

Synapse doesn't teach from one persona. When you're in the transformers phase, he loads a transformer specialist. Deployment phase — different expert. He's always relevant because he's never generic.

04
Start where it matters

Before lesson one, Synapse runs a placement check. You enter where your knowledge actually is, not where someone assumed you'd start. No wasted sessions grinding through things you already know.

05
Generic AI tutor trap

Codecademy with a chatbot doesn't build understanding, it builds dependency. A tool that explains the answer doesn't teach you to find it.

06
Toy project syndrome

Build a calculator. Build a to-do app. Build something you'll never use. Every capstone here connects to what you're actually working on.

07
Outdated by design

AI moves quarterly. Most courses were written for a market that no longer exists. This curriculum is reviewed against what's shipping, not what shipped.

08
Death by tab

A dozen sources, zero coherence. Browser graveyards and no mental model. One path. One sequence. One place to be.
