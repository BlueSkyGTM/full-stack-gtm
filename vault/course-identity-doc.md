# Course Identity

**Course name:** BlueSkyGTM Engineering

**Origin:** "Blue sky" research is theoretical work done without a specific application in mind — the kind of thinking that happens before anyone knows it matters. The name comes from a period of unemployment, reading about context window management, and working out theories about how FAANG teams handled AI context — with no job, no credentials, and no idea the field was about to explode. It turned out to matter. This curriculum is built for people in that same position: avid, self-taught, not yet credentialed, and more capable than the market currently recognizes.

**Thesis:** You can't be a complete GTM engineer without understanding AI engineering. And if you get AI engineering right, you can do almost everything else in GTM from first principles.

**Positioning:** GTM Engineering with a technical foundation. Not AI with GTM slapped on it. Not GTM with AI slapped on it. The GTM engineer who owns the entire revenue stack from signal detection through AI infrastructure through outbound execution. The domain `blueskygtm.engineer` is not aspirational — it is the job title the student earns.

**Why the name removes ambiguity:** Centers the GTM engineer identity, not the AI tools. Students walk away with a job title, not a tool certification.

## Design Decisions (locked)

**Who this is built for first:** A single practitioner using themselves as the guinea pig. The curriculum is validated by building it, running it, and shipping the live system — not by focus groups.

**The real career thesis:** 80/20 GTM engineer with AI engineering crossover optionality. Not "be the best GTM engineer." Not "be an AI engineer who does GTM." The goal is: if the GTM tool stack turns over (it will), you cross into AI engineering. If the AI hype cycle corrects, you're already a working GTM engineer. The MLOps appendage is not extra credit — it is the hedge instrument. Most GTM engineers stop at deployment. Knowing how to monitor, version, and retrain a model is how you outlive the people who were in it for the trends.

**Why the AI engineering depth is justified:** Phases 01-20 teach more than what the 2026 GTM job market requires today. That is intentional. The market rewards Clay certifications now. In two years it will reward people who understand what Clay is actually doing under the hood. This curriculum is built for where the market is going, not where it is.

**The GTM handbook provenance:** The 80/20 GTM Engineering Playbook was sourced from a six-figure GTM engineer running a community of other six-figure GTM engineers. The enterprise layer (Section 4) represents current advanced market practice, not aspirational content. The techniques and tools in the handbook are what practitioners are billing $8K–$15K/month retainers to deliver.

**Platform (locked):** Claude Code Desktop. The student has one environment — the terminal. No IDE, no browser-dependent exercises, no tool switching. Every exercise is completable inside Claude Code without leaving the shell. This is not a constraint; it is the point. The course teaches the way modern AI engineers actually work.

**What this is not:** Workplace superiority. Credential stacking. Putting eggs in two sexy baskets. It is a compound discipline built to be durable.

**This repo is two things:** A course and a coursemaker. The course is BlueSkyGTM Engineering — the content, the 502 lessons, the GTM handbook integration. The coursemaker is everything else: the ICM build pipeline (Stages 01-10), the quality evaluation layer (CLARITY/WEAVE/ACCURACY judges), the illustration pipeline (GLM-image/Excalidraw/Mermaid), and the Helix governed-maze architecture. The coursemaker is designed to be domain-agnostic. The GTM content is one instantiation. For others building technical courses with a hybrid AI/application structure, the coursemaker may be the more valuable artifact.

**Why the GTM content is accurate by construction:** The AI engineering curriculum is the reasoning chain. Every GTM lesson has to answer "what does this specific technical concept let you do in GTM?" — it cannot drift into vague best practices because the AI concept anchors it. GTM-first curricula are opinions all the way down. This curriculum has a validated technical spine across 502 lessons that forces every GTM claim to be grounded in a specific mechanism. The Double Helix doesn't just organize the content — it enforces accuracy as a structural property.
